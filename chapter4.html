CHAPTER FOUR

The Talk

Written by Nizkar Storm

"Ready, set, and... go," Taimen counted down lazily, instantly initiating a high-speed sprint across the open ground between Crimson-Shock and Lumen. Confidently surging forward, Crimson taunted mockingly, "Can't see you behind me!" firmly believing he had left her far in the dust. "Because I'm already in front of you, fool," Lumen replied calmly, leaning against the finish line structure. Staring in absolute shock, Crimson muttered, "You really do travel at the speed of light."

Meanwhile, Moondust and Hazel had just finished hauling supplies down into their subterranean bunker. The hideout was now stocked with critical research papers recovered from Crimson’s old apartment, specialized detective tools from Lumen’s house—courtesy of her father's work as an elite archaeologist—and a fresh stockpile of snacks they had bought across town.

"Umm, guys... did one of you put this note here?" Hazel asked, her eyes narrowing as she lifted a piece of parchment from the central table. Lumen looked over suspiciously, "What?"

On the other side of the room, Kage nudged Nathan's shoulder. "Hey, Sparky, why didn't you tell Taimen to help carry any of this heavy equipment?" Nathan sighed grimly, "We both know the answer to that." Resting on a nearby crate, Taimen was already drifting off, silently calculating how many weeks remained until final exams so he could finally enjoy some peace and quiet.

Hazel cleared her throat and read the handwritten ink out loud: "Aurelius writing here. I have observed that many of you still carry deep, unanswered questions regarding the elements. Furthermore, I have just intercepted some leaked intelligence tracking the dark operational grid of the Achelments, and it is imperative that you review it. Write your specific inquiries down. I shall manifest in your bunker tomorrow evening at exactly 6:00 PM."

Hazel jumped up excitedly, "Yippee! Our questions are finally getting answered!" Taimen opened one lazy eye, muttering flatly, "Since when did that celestial god become a pen-pal writer?" Crimson grinned under his breath, "Honestly, I'm trying to figure out when he became a secret detective." The entire underground room erupted into laughter, the heavy tension of the incoming war fading away for just a moment.

The plan was clear, and everyone knew exactly what to do. However, Hazel paused, looking around the room. "Should we prepare something special to welcome a god?" Kira nodded in agreement. "You're right. At the very least, we should clean up this bunker, because Kage over here completely forgot his chores." Kage flashed a grin, trying to look like an innocent kid. "Alright, let's meet back here at 5:00 PM tomorrow then," Nathan finalized. With that settled, the rest of the evening went by like any ordinary day, filled with anticipation for what was to come.

The next afternoon, by 4:52 PM, only Kira and Hazel had arrived at the hideout. Taimen materialized exactly at 5:00 PM on the dot. Looking around the messy room, Kira sighed, "Since Nathan and Kage are running late, we had better get to work ourselves." Taimen stepped forward, manifesting his powers. "Shadow Fingers," he muttered flatly. Long, flexible tendrils of dark shadow surged from the floorboards, quickly fluffing and straightening the stray pillows. Simultaneously, Hazel activated her gravity manipulation, using a localized Gravity Lift to levitate the broom and sweep the dust out of the bunker seamlessly. Kira shifted into her Riptide form, generating a controlled water stream to flawlessly mop and refresh the underground floors.

Just as they finished, Nathan and Kage finally burst through the bunker entrance. "Hey guys, look! We found some room spray," Nathan announced proudly, holding up a bottle. Hazel threw her hands up, screaming in mock frustration, "Is that seriously what took you two so long?!" Kage looked around the spotless room, completely amazed. "Ooh, you guys did a really incredible job cleaning this place up. By the way, Nathan and I actually thought of a brilliant name for our base during our walk." Taimen, sounding genuinely interested for the first time, sat up. "What is it then?"

"Elementox," Nathan declared confidently. Kira smiled positively, "Not bad at all." Nathan checked his watch. "It's 5:30 PM now. We need to start brainstorming our questions before the timer hits zero." Over the next half hour, the five teenagers gathered around the central table, carefully mapping out a massive, comprehensive list of inquiries for the deity of light.

The clock finally struck exactly 6:00 PM, and everyone sat up perfectly straight, bracing themselves to welcome Aurelius. "Keep your guard up. Acheron could easily try to ambush us under this guise," Nathan warned sharply, shifting instantly into his Blaze form as he wrapped a crackling, fiery chain around his wrist. Right on cue, a thick, luminous blue fog spread rapidly across the floorboards, accompanied by an eerie, echoing celestial melody. He was here—the primordial god deity, Aurelius. Every single Aurement immediately bowed in deep respect. "Rise and sit straight, my young warriors. Do not interrupt me while I speak," the deity commanded. Kage leaned over, murmuring quietly to Kira, "Wait, I thought we were the ones who were going to ask the questions." "I HEARD THAT!" Aurelius boomed, his tone shifting to sudden irritation. The room went completely silent as the teenagers sat frozen. Aurelius cleared his throat and continued:

"The cosmic war has officially begun, and I must admit, you all look remarkably prepared. I have intercepted highly critical intelligence to share with my vanguard," Aurelius explained, his voice softening into a kind, guiding tone. "There are a total of six high-tier Achelments operating in this sector. Their absolute primary objective is to locate and capture Lyra." Hearing this, Nathan’s mind raced with absolute fury. When he had faced Terra-Voss in their brutal duel, the villain had teased a hidden secondary motive for the invasion—now, the missing piece of the puzzle had finally clicked into place. "The first operative is the earthen commander you already know as Terra-Voss. However, that is merely a fraction of his arsenal. He possesses a sub-elemental mastery over metal, operating under the heavy vanguard title of Titan." The team gasped in shock; they had never anticipated that the Achelments wielded dual-power structures too.

"Furthermore, he is not dead," Aurelius revealed. "The dark forces bestowed three distinct lives upon him; he has burned through two, leaving him with one final resurrection. "Forgive the interruption, Lord Aurelius, but how does he possess three lives while we only have one?" Nathan interjected, his volcanic aura flaring. Aurelius sighed gently, "Unfortunately, I do not hold the authority to grant recursive life force. Now, moving to Death-Vine—he is also active. He survived your encounter not through extra lives, but because his cellular structure controls organic matter and is entirely biodegradable." "Then how do we slate a monster like that?" Taimen asked, his shadow tendrils tensing. "To permanently neutralize him, you must destroy his two vital cores—the brain and the heart—at the exact same microsecond. Finally, there is an elite infiltrator you may cross paths with very soon. Sanguine is a female operative commanding lethal blood manipulation, but she also wields a secondary sand element under the desert codename Sahara. Her true cosmic name is Nitmeia."

"Whoa, hold on!" Nova-Pull exclaimed, raising her hand high. "How come the villains get double powers while only Nathan and Kira have them? That was literally number one on our question list!" Aurelius looked down at Nathan solemnly. "You do not yet understand the severe physical toll placed upon Nathan. When balancing both elements, he possesses the lowest raw Eloxer reserve among you all. This Eloxer energy determines the destructive output of an elemental strike. Because I had limited energy left to distribute after empowering the rest of you, he received two forms but a smaller fuel tank. However, I granted them to him because I knew his martial arts discipline and tactical intellect could bridge the deficit." Kira looked down, nodding silently; her tactical Visi-Glasses had already calculated that Nathan's base energy signals were running much lower than the rest of the squad's. "As for Kira," Aurelius concluded, his eyes shining with pride, "she earned her dual affinity because I could find no soul in the cosmos more resilient or capable of anchoring the weight of the ancient Riptide stream."

"So you're saying that there were actual people who wielded these exact powers before us?" Shadow exclaimed, his jaw dropping as the shadow tendrils around him rippled in surprise. "Yes, on entirely different planets across distant, forgotten galaxies," Aurelius explained smoothly. "Once their cosmic duty is fully completed, the elemental powers are stripped away and taken back to the source." "What?! Which planets? Did they wield the exact same elemental types as us, and did every single one of them win their wars?" Moondust fired off, h eyes wide with curiosity. Aurelius offered a rare, knowing smile. "There have been only three generations of Aurements in existence before your cycle. And yes, against all odds, every single one of them emerged victorious."

"How could there only be three generations before us when the universe has existed for trillions of years?" Blaze interjected, his fiery aura flaring slightly with deep concern over the mathematical impossibility. Aurelius’s smile instantly vanished, his eyes flashing with a stern, blinding light. "You do not need to know any more on this specific topic. Now, where was I? Ah, yes—I had just finished detailing the threat of Nitmeia," the deity stated strictly, his booming tone making it crystal clear that nobody should dare interrupt him again. The teenagers froze, locking their lips tightly. Aurelius leaned forward, his voice dropping to a cold, ominous whisper. "Furthermore, the next operative you must brace yourselves for is Brecify."

"He possesses wind powers and is exceptionally formidable—honestly, his threat level might scale exactly as high as Terra-Voss. I do not hold much strategic intelligence on his background yet, but I know for a fact he only commands that single element," Aurelius detailed. "Now, onto the final Achelment operative. The most... disadvantaged of the circle, if I may put it that way, is Gloom-Summoner." The team shared highly shocked and confused looks. "She possesses the rare ability to summon void-born crawlers. However, the reason she is treated as an outcast by her peers is her dangerously low Eloxer reserve. To compensate for her lack of raw energy, she heavily relies on an arsenal of tactical weapons for physical combat." Lumen's eyes suddenly widened behind her Visi-Glasses. "Wait... if her Eloxer signal is that low, what if she is spying on us right now and our sensors can't pick her up?" "About that..." Blaze muttered smoothly. The entire room gasped at the sight before them. Wrapped tightly within Nathan's crackling, blazing thermal chains was Gloom-Summoner herself—she had infiltrated the perimeter and was hiding deep inside the bunker.

"Please, just let me go! I swear I mean no real harm!" Gloom-Summoner pleaded, wincing against the heat of the binding links. "Yeah, right. We might consider letting you slide—but only if you spill every piece of dark intel we want to hear," Blaze countered confidently, tightening his grip on the fiery restraints. Aurelius looked down, visibly impressed by the young leader's reflexes. "Release her, Nathan. You cannot execute an opponent in the direct presence of a celestial deity. It goes entirely against the protocols." "What protocols? What rules?!" Moondust demanded, throwing her hands up. Gloom-Summoner let out a malicious, mocking laugh. "You pathetic little rookies don't even know what the grand cosmic rules are? Wow. I completely assumed the mighty Aurelius would have briefed his little lambs before dragging you into this meeting! You were so busy looking at the sky that you didn't realize I've been tracking you this entire time, spawning gloomers right under your noses while my low Eloxer kept me completely invisible."

"Don't you dare get me started on having low energy reserves," Gloom-Summoner sneered, locking her venomous eyes directly onto Nathan. "Look who's talking about running on empty, Mr. Low-Reserves." That was the absolute final straw. As an infuriated Blaze instantly began charging a massive, volatile fireball in his palm, the infiltrator triggered her trap. In a split second, she summoned 500 massive, chittering Gloom Crawlers right outside the bunker walls, fracturing the base defense grid! Simultaneously, a single giant crawler ruptured through the floorboards right beside her, tearing Nathan's thermal chains apart and breaking her restraints. Leaping onto the monster's back, she cackled wildly over the alarms. "Now I'm free, and I'll flee!"

"Let her go and focus entirely on protecting the bunker's structural integrity. My presence here has concluded—but before I depart, hear the official cosmic power rankings for your cycle: Hazel sits at the absolute bottom. Taimen follows closely behind her. Kage is formidable, but lacks the explosive depth of the others. Kira holds the definitive second place position. As for the rest, you can easily deduce the hierarchy yourselves," Aurelius commanded coolly, his divine light fracturing as he dissolved back into the stellar grid.

Everyone stood frozen for a split second, thinking, 'What the heck was that timing?!' But with the alarms blaring, there was no time to argue. "Nova-Pull and Shadow, you two handle the front gate vector!" Nathan barked, stepping into his tactical commander role. "Moondust, Blaze, and I will split up to secure the remaining three parameters!" Sprinting up the metal steps, Blaze leaped onto the bunker roof, instantly unleashing a rapid-fire barrage of volcanic fireballs down onto the swarming gloomers. The massive thermal explosions completely incinerated his entire quadrant and instantly wiped out half of Moondust's sector. "Seriously, dude? Why do you have to steal all the targets?!" Moondust groaned, his celestial energy fizzling out. Meanwhile, Lumen calculated the geometric trajectories below, charging forward and cleanly slicing through a wave of monsters with a single, high-frequency flash of her cosmic light rod.

Over on the front lines, Nova grinned as her gravitational aura flared. Locking onto a massive, rusted bulldozer abandoned near the construction site, she hoisted the multi-ton vehicle directly into the air with Nova-Pull and launched it like a missile, crushing nearly half of her attacking wave in a single impact. Shadow seamlessly covered the remaining flank, sending his massive, dark shadow-tiger roaring through the smoke to rip apart the stragglers. Within minutes, the battlefield fell completely silent. "Aurements victorious," Blaze declared, powering down his thermal chains with a confident smirk. "Yeah, great, we survived. Now what?" Moondust muttered, dusting off her uniform. Hazel instantly reverted back to her human state, crossing her arms with a fierce pout. "I'll tell you 'now what'—we can talk about literally anything else in the universe right now, except for those stupid god-tier rankings!"
