CHAPTER TWO

The First Battle and A Victory

Written by Nizkar Storm

"Beware!"—the single word was partially haunting Nathan, yet it also hyped him up. Since that night, he had been practicing his powers relentlessly. Crimson-Shock was doing pretty well, but Blaze, on the other hand, hadn't exactly come out to say hello yet. Still, Nathan felt confident that he was ready to fight whenever he encountered another monster.

In fact, he had already fought fifty-six monsters since Acheron's warning. He officially named these creatures "Gloom Crawlers," classifying them into four distinct types: Huge, Large, Medium, and Small. Nathan observed that the weaker ones usually attacked in massive packs of forty to fifty, which Nathan slayed entirely on his own, while the huge variants only arrived in pairs of one or two.

Through all of this, Nathan kept a strict rule: he didn't let a single soul know about his powers. Fortunately, the Gloom Crawlers always seemed to spawn only when he was completely alone, meaning they hadn't caused massive public destruction or killed anyone yet. Nathan kept meditating on two theories regarding the upcoming war: either it was going to be a massive army of these beasts, or Acheron would send another human gifted with dark powers to attack him.

He had just come home after finishing his mid-term exams, and they went awesomely. He then, as usual, headed straight out to his secret training ground. When Nathan suddenly felt the atmospheric pressure shift, his analytical mind knew it was going to start raining in approximately twenty minutes. His normal practice session usually lasted two hours. Ten minutes passed while Nathan was still stretching, and the sky turned completely dark and cloudy. Exactly as predicted, it began raining heavily over the next ten minutes.

While anyone else would have packed up and left, Nathan wasn't like other people. He remained completely undisturbed for two specific reasons: he wanted to stay strictly consistent, and he wanted to test if his thunder powers would be affected by the rain. He knew for a fact that water was an excellent conductor of electricity. He dug up a small patch of dirt and let the heavy rainfall fill the hole into a puddle.

Before he could test touching the pool of water—since he was still unable to shoot thunder out of his hands in a ranged attacking way—one of the Gloom Crawlers suddenly spawned. Crimson-Shock smiled under his breath and muttered, "Just in time."

Noticing a stone well nearby, he sprinted over, grabbed the wooden bucket, and filled it with water. He dashed back and splashed the water completely over the monster. While the creature was temporarily disoriented, Crimson-Shock moved swiftly behind it. He charged up the scarlet electricity in his hand, reached out, and laid his palm flat against the wet skin of the crawler. Sparkling red thunder surged violently through the beast for three straight seconds before it completely exploded into particles.

Crimson-Shock brushed himself off. "Too late to say goodbye now, I guess." He finished the rest of his intense practice session and finally headed back home, feeling incredibly proud of his experiment.

However, the moment he opened the front door, his mother yelled angrily, "Where on earth were you?! You are officially grounded for one whole week!"

Nathan's heart sank as he walked up to his room. He thought to himself in a panic, "How am I supposed to carry on my practice now?" Now, Nathan wasn't really a rule-breaker kind of boy, mostly because he had never found a reason to be. But skipping his elemental control practice just because he was grounded wasn't an option. On the bright side, Nathan thought that at least his mother cared enough about him to discipline him. He briefly considered telling her about his superpowers, but she wasn't exactly the most trustworthy or understanding person, so that option was a definitive no.

Nathan had no choice left: he had to sneak out of his bedroom window. The next day after returning from school, he carefully planned his escape. Both of his parents were currently in their bedroom taking an afternoon nap. "Great," Nathan thought. The only remaining problem was his brother, who had just woken up. Acting quickly, Nathan struck a covert deal with him using two bars of chocolate as a bribe. He then stuffed a body pillow and his regular pillow under the blanket to create a fake replica of himself, and slipped out into the afternoon.

He had made it out—well, partially, since he could still be caught if his brother broke the truce or if his parents checked on him, though they almost never did. He sprinted until he reached his training ground, where a pack of twenty-five blood-thirsty, Medium-sized Gloom Crawlers were already waiting for him. Nathan groaned, "Come on, give me a break, will ya?"

The monsters charged simultaneously. Crimson-Shock immediately used his tactical brain, identifying a perfect zig-zag pattern to blitz them from the farthest to the nearest targets, swiftly slaying every single one of them. While their bodies began their rapid process of decay, he pushed through the rest of his grueling practice session before heading back home.

Distracted by his training, Nathan stupidly reached out to ring his own front doorbell before suddenly freezing as he remembered he was supposed to be locked inside. Panicking, he scrambled back up through his bedroom window. The second he stepped inside, his mother’s voice boomed up the stairs: "NATHAN!"

Nathan froze, completely creeped out, assuming he was caught. But his mother simply followed up with, "Eat your dinner, fast!" Nathan let out a massive sigh of relief, collapsing onto his bed. "Talk about good timing," he thought.

Wednesday was officially the worst day of the week for Nathan, because every week on this dreadful day, he had mandatory dance class. To make matters worse, it was scheduled right after lunch break. This was a major tactical problem for Nathan; he wanted to maintain peak physical fitness, and engaging in intense, serious movement immediately after eating was definitely not the way to do it.

Moreover, the dance teacher absolutely detested him because of his notoriously low energy while dancing. There was really nothing Nathan could do about it today, though—he had just eaten a incredibly heavy peanut butter and jelly sandwich, and his stomach was weighing him down.

As the class formed a single line and slowly moved towards the dance room, they felt a violent, sudden shake beneath the floorboards. Seconds later, the school P.A. system crackled to life, announcing that an earthquake had just occurred and ordering all classes to immediately evacuate to the sports playing field since it was a wide-open ground.

At first, Nathan truly believed it was just a normal natural disaster. It was a massive mistake for him to think that. While the rest of the frightened school walked quickly past him to safety, Nathan stayed focused, silently wondering when he would finally get the perfect opportunity to test out his brand-new elemental combat moves. He was about to find out a lot sooner than he expected.

Nathan was walking at the very back of the crowd when he suddenly heard a collective, loud gasp from the teachers at the front of the line. He immediately sprinted ahead to investigate. To his absolute shock, thousands of Gloom Crawlers were bursting out from fissures in the ground. Nathan didn't have a problem with their sheer numbers; his real panic was that he would have to reveal his secret identity to the entire school.

But with lives on the line, he had no choice. The best he could do was find something to wrap around his face. Moving with lightning speed, he dashed back into the dance room, grabbed a discarded fabric prop, and tied it securely around his head, covering everything except his eyes. Crimson-Shock was confident he could defeat these beasts blindfolded, but he couldn't risk a single innocent life.

He instantly reappeared out on the sports field, standing tall and confident. The evacuated teachers and students were utterly shocked. They could tell it was a student by his size, but they had no idea who he was or why he was wearing a makeshift mask. More than anything, everyone wondered how he had just seemingly teleported out of thin air.

Crimson-Shock taunted the horde, yelling, "Your so-called god said he would send something terrifying down my path! Is this really all he's got?!" The Gloom Crawlers charged at him angrily, acting highly offended by the insult.

As they closed in, Crimson-Shock remembered his brand-new defensive technique. He turned his back to the crowd, extending his open palms toward the students and teachers as if pushing against an invisible force. Brilliant blue circles of light manifested around his hands, and a massive energy wall erupted from the earth, curving over the crowd into a gigantic, protective dome-shaped barrier. Crimson-Shock smirked behind his mask. "The only ones dying today will be you."

In the blink of an eye, a massive wave of scarlet energy rippled outward, instantly vaporizing ninety percent of the monster horde. He casually electrocuted the remaining stragglers by plainly touching them. But just as the field cleared, ten Huge-class Gloom Crawlers spawned into reality. Nathan analyzed them quickly: "It's going to be way too hard to slash them like the smaller ones since their skin is super thick."

Focusing the entirety of his Crimson-Shock electricity into his legs, he executed a massive, soaring jump that launched him up to the height of the school building. Looking down upon the giants, he roared, "Thunder Rain!"

A cataclysmic storm of bright red thunderbolts rained down from above, violently piercing through the thick skin of all ten giants and destroying them instantly. Crimson-Shock drifted smoothly back down to the grass, casually dusting off his hands. Inside the translucent energy dome, the students began whispering excitedly among themselves: "Whatever those things were, that red kid just saved us flawlessly!"

Crimson-Shock crossed his arms and yelled back, "Hey, I'm no 'red kid'. My name is Crimson-Shock!" Every eye in the crowd glared at him in absolute awe. Feeling proud and genuinely respected for the very first time in his life, Nathan began scanning the crowd inside the dome, only to suddenly realise that five students were missing. He knew for a fact that four people from his high school were absent today. He had been carefully noting this because he knew that once the real war waged, he would have to keep strict track of everyone. "Where is the fifth student?" he asked himself suspiciously.

In no time at all, a massive crack ripped open in the center of the sports field. Rising up from the fissure came a boy dressed in a striking brown-and-purple outfit, calmly sitting upon a throne made of solid stone. While his outfit design closely mirrored Crimson-Shock's own warrior style, Nathan knew instantly that this newcomer couldn't possibly be an ally. The dark, heavy energy radiating from him was the exact opposite forces Nathan had studied in his room.

The earth-powered boy smiled calmly. "Looks like my army has been defeated. No matter; I am ten times stronger than all of them combined. My name is Terra-Voss—or as you classmates know me, Flint." Nathan's eyes widened. He recognised Flint; he was a student in a different class section, but in the exact same grade level.

Terra-Voss sneered, locking eyes with him. "I've been sent here for two distinct reasons. The first is to take absolute revenge on you for rejecting my god... Nathan."

Crimson-Shock completely froze. Behind him, the protected students erupted into a wave of confused whispers. Was this legendary elemental warrior really the quiet, isolated Nathan? Hearing his secret completely exposed, Crimson-Shock reached up and untied the fabric. "Now that you all know exactly who I am, I have no reason to wear this anymore," he declared, letting the mask drop to the grass.

Terra-Voss stepped down from his earth throne, his eyes shifting away from Nathan and targeting a terrified girl named Aria standing near the edge of the crowd. He charged forward, penetrating through Nathan's blue force field without any resistance at all.

Nathan wasn't about to let this slide. Moving with near-teleportation speed, he intercepted the villain right in front of Aria, catching and blocking Terra-Voss's heavy stone-infused strike with a manifestation of his own crackling thunderbolt. Terra-Voss let out a heavy grunt of frustration, but Nathan quickly dropped his weapon, swiveled his hips, and delivered a devastating, lightning-fast front kick straight into the villain's chest.

Crimson-Shock pulled his thunderbolt back into a ready combat pose, and Terra-Voss did the exact same with his own weapon. Flint's weapon looked like a massive, straight pole made of solid rock, with jagged mountain spikes protruding fiercely out of its sides. Both elemental warriors charged at each other at blinding speeds, colliding in a strike that released a massive wave of raw energy across the sports field.

An epic weapon battle erupted between them, their hands moving with such rapid, intricate precision that none of the watching students could read the strikes; however, everyone could easily tell that Crimson-Shock was completely overpowering him. Frustrated, Terra-Voss smashed his open palm into the ground. A massive, towering pillar of rock erupted violently from beneath the turf, launching Crimson-Shock high into the sky.

While airborne, Nathan rapidly threw a thunderbolt in front of him, behind him, and to both of his sides. Finally, he threw a fifth thunderbolt right next to the villain. Terra-Voss looked around at the scattered weapons and yelled tauntingly, "Ha! You can't even aim right!"

Crimson-Shock dropped down heavily right where he had thrown the first bolt, ignoring the insult. He grabbed a fresh lightning bolt in his palm and rubbed it fiercely against the grounded weapon. In an instant, a massive electric current chained outward, and all five scattered thunderbolts began passing a cataclysmic amount of electricity between each other, forming a lethal trap. Terra-Voss was caught dead in the center and got brutally electrocuted by the crossfire. Crimson-Shock looked at him and replied coldly, "Bull's eye." Terra-Voss let out a painful groan and fell heavily to his knees.

While everyone else in the crowd thought Crimson-Shock had won, both Kage and Nathan knew it wasn't over yet. They could still feel heavy energy waves radiating across the field—one force attracting and the other aggressively opposing Nathan's own elemental aura. Suddenly, Terra-Voss collapsed backward and sank directly inside the earth.

"What now?" Crimson-Shock muttered, scanning the horizon. His eyes locked onto a nearby apartment tower visible from the school grounds. Massive, jagged rocky spikes were erupting violently from the building. In a flash of horror, Nathan realised that was his own home. He took off, running faster than he ever had in his entire life toward the apartment. When he arrived, his heart shattered; he could see his parents' and his brother's bodies impaled through the stone spikes, struck right through the heart.

He found Terra-Voss standing on the rooftop, laughing like a complete maniac. "Who is more powerful now?!" the villain jeered.

Fueled by pure, unadulterated rage, Nathan's aura shifted completely. His outfit transformed, bursting into a fierce, fiery color palette. It had finally happened—he was now officially manifested as Blaze. Blaze flew forward, grabbed Terra-Voss tightly by his collar, and threw him with such immense power that the villain crashed all the way back onto the school grounds.

Back at the school, every student was completely terrified as the blue force field began breaking down, allowing the remaining Gloom Crawlers to close in. Blaze descended from the sky. Blinded by rage, he hurled ten massive fireballs straight at the villain. Terra-Voss barely managed to slam up a solid rock barrier just in time to absorb the explosions. Seizing a tactical opportunity, Blaze shattered the school's power generator, reverted back into Crimson-Shock, and absorbed the raw electrical grid energy into his palms, condensing it into a rapidly spinning sphere. Terra-Voss cracked the ground beneath him, absorbing the raw tectonic forces and sharp rocks into his own ultimate attack.

While the two titans were charging up, the surrounding Gloom Crawlers suddenly began decaying into ash. Out of the shadows, Kage had finally taken his true warrior form as Moondust. He conjured a glowing, celestial sphere resembling the moon, sending powerful moonbeams piercing through every single monster on the field. The watching students remained in absolute shock.

This was it. Crimson-Shock and Terra-Voss charged at one another, resulting in a cataclysmic explosion. Thick smoke and debris spread everywhere. Moondust reverted back into his normal form, squinting through the haze, unable to see anything. Finally, a sharp gust of wind blew the dust away. Terra-Voss's physical body completely broke apart into lifeless pieces of rock, while Crimson-Shock remained standing, clutching a heavily bleeding arm.

The entire school cheered loudly in relief. The frantic teachers rushed onto the field, carefully picking up a exhausted Nathan and bringing him straight to the MedBay. It was officially over; Crimson-Shock had won. The chaotic school day finally concluded, and the students boarded their buses to head home.

Before leaving the school, Kage stopped by Nathan's medical cot. Looking down at his friend, he murmured, "Well, that's certainly one way to skip dance class." Nathan was completely unconscious, so he couldn't hear him. Kage's expression turned somber. "If only I had fought alongside you from the start, your family might still be alive. Bye for now." With that, he departed into the afternoon, and thus began their fateful summer vacation.
