CHAPTER ONE

The Right Holder Of Great Power

Written by Nizkar Storm

Nathan woke with a sudden jerk, the sharp rattling of the school bus pulling him from a heavy sleep. He stared out the window, completely shocked—he had dozed off, and his stop had already arrived. As usual, the heavy shadows of Adrian and Marcus loomed over his seat. They immediately began their mocking routine, but Nathan refused to give them the satisfaction of a reaction. He stepped off the bus and calmly crossed the road, blocking out the cruel jeers echoing behind him: "Look, Mr. Important is taking his time!" Cruel laughter erupted from the bus windows, but Nathan kept his eyes fixed forward. To the rest of the world, he was just a quiet, invisible seventh-grader who kept his head down.

When he reached his apartment tower, he stood before the broken elevator, waiting ten whole minutes for the lift. He didn't mind the delay; there was no one waiting for him at home anyway, his parents also thought of him as a creep. Inside his quiet apartment, he moved through his routine like a ghost—changing his clothes and quickly eating a grilled chicken wrap. He lay reluctantly on his bed, staring at the ceiling for thirty minutes before pulling out his notebook. He finished the entire day's homework in under five minutes. The bullies saw an easy target, but they had no idea of the truth: Nathan was dangerously smart. He went down for cycling in the evening and came back to have dinner. His daily routine wasn't very exciting, but then again, he didn't care.

When the next morning arrived, he dressed for school and boarded the bus as it pulled up. This time, Adrian and Marcus weren't focused on Nathan; they were busy making fun of the popular girl, Lyra. That hit a nerve for Nathan. He stood up in the aisle. Adrian burst out laughing, stepping forward. "Well, well, well, do you wanna mess with me?"

Nathan replied calmly, "Not really scared of you."

The bus went completely silent. No one had ever stood up to Adrian or Marcus before. Nathan followed up with a cold stare: "Being in eleventh grade and messing with a seventh grader. Very mature of you."

Furious, Adrian drove a heavy punch straight at his face. Nathan, using the back of his hand, calmly deflected the blow. In a split second, he grabbed Adrian's arm and slammed him hard onto the bus floor. The entire bus erupted into loud cheers—except for Marcus, who was suddenly trembling, worried he would be next. But Lyra just laughed, turning to her friend. "Haha, look, he became Mr. Prince instead of Mr. Important."

Nathan felt a sudden sting of embarrassment. After all, he had just saved her, and this was how he was paid back. The rest of the school day dragged on like any other, right up until the dead of night, when something weird happened.

A mysterious, ash-grey mist began to seep into the corners of his room, accompanied by the faint, echoing strain of eerie music growing closer to Nathan’s ears. Terrified but alert, he sat rigid in his bed, his senses scanning the darkness just in case something lunged forward. Then, without a sound, a towering blue deity materialized before him. Inside the god's translucent form, a sea of infinite stars shifted silently—it was as if the entity held the entire weight of the universe inside his chest.

Nathan managed to sit upright, demanding in a shaken voice, "Who are you? What do you want?"

"I am Aurelius," the deity replied, his voice resonating like a subterranean echo. "An internal deity possessing primordial elemental forces, sent to hand them down to a chosen few. I do not have much time, so I will make this brief."

"Why?" Nathan interrupted, his mind racing.

Aurelius offered a grim smile. "My twin brother, Acheron, has surrendered to the dark side. He is actively shattering planets across the cosmos. Because he cannot manifest his cosmic powers directly on your world, he deploys deadly organisms and grants them his fraction of power. You humans are in grave, immediate danger. Therefore, I grant you the dominion over electrifying thunder and blazing fire."

Nathan stared at his own trembling hands. "But why me? I am nothing."

Aurelius offered a brief glance of raw courage. "I saw what you did on that bus today. There are very few who possess your resolve. You have allies in this fight, and they are not far from you. Find them."

With a sudden, deafening static sound, the deity vanished into thin air. Shaken to his core, Nathan tried his best to force himself back to sleep, desperate to ignore the impossible truth. When he woke the next morning, the sun was shining, and he tried to convince himself it had all been a bizarre dream.

He got up, still partially hallucinating from the night before. Today was Sunday, so he ate breakfast and rode his cycle down to a completely open ground—the same cycle his little brother had been trying to break ever since Nathan got it. This ground barely had any people walking on it, making it Nathan's favourite place to go whenever he wanted to be left alone.

Nathan had confused feelings, torn between whether he wanted to accept the fact that he possessed supernatural powers, or if he was just having a crazy dream and couldn't handle such a massive responsibility. He reversed the deity's saying over and over in his head, trying to find a loophole.

In the meantime, a ball came flying straight at Nathan's head. He dodged it in the blink of an eye and caught it mid-air. Turns out, it was Marcus trying to get cheap revenge. But the moment Nathan caught the ball, Marcus got terrified and ran away the next second, yelling, "Creep!"

Was it true? Nathan didn't even know himself. Deciding to accept his fate, he focused his energy and tried releasing sparks from his fingertips. Suddenly, out of nowhere, he was holding a solid red thunderbolt emitting a wave of huge energy. That was not all. His clothes instantly transformed into a black cap with a red thunderbolt sign, a cut-sleeves jacket with thundering patterns over a black full-sleeve t-shirt, and very baggy black jeans ending in glowing red shoes.

He was in dismay, yet partially overjoyed. He muttered to himself, "What kind of creatures will I fight? Have my allies discovered their powers? How many allies do I have? Where is my fire power?" He had a whole stock of questions, but unfortunately, no one to answer them.

He started spinning his thunderbolt like a professional, practicing how to handle it like a sword. He threw it forward like a trident, and when he went to go retrieve it, he instantly found himself standing right next to the thunderbolt. He knew it then—he was also as fast as lightning. When he let go of the thunderbolt, his body started emitting low sparks. Focusing his full concentration to turn back to normal, his magical gear vanished, leaving him in his ordinary clothes again.

He rode his cycle back home, pulled out a pen and paper, and mapped out exactly what he had to take care of while wielding these forces. He wrote down three strict rules:

  • LET MY POWERS BE A SECRET
  • I WILL NOT USE THIS ON SOMEONE FOR REVENGE
  • I WILL PRACTICE DAILY TO GAIN CONTROL OVER MY POWERS

With that done, Nathan felt a new wave of discipline settle over him, sharper than ever before.

He woke up the next day, but it was unfortunately Monday, so he would have to wait until later to visit the open ground. He dressed up and headed to school with a slightly more active mood. To his absolute shock, everyone in his classroom was waiting for him just to cheer for what he had done to Adrian the other day. He was more overjoyed than ever before. Everyone came over to talk to him, including a boy named Kage.

Nathan chatted with everyone, but when he spoke with Kage, he felt a strange sensation deep inside himself. He shook it off, sure it was nothing. Kage seemed like a pretty nice guy—handsome, top of the class in studies just like Nathan, and great at playing football. Nathan immediately decided he wanted to be good friends with him. Still, Nathan kept one strict rule in mind throughout the entire school day: he could not tell a single soul about his superpowers.

Once the school day finally ended, he headed straight home, relieved to see no sign of Adrian or Marcus anywhere near him. He ate lunch, rested for a bit, and then eagerly headed off for his evening cycling session at the open ground. The moment he arrived, he transformed into his powerful, reddish alter-ego. He brainstormed a complex, cool name for his new identity. "Crimson-Shock, that's it!" Nathan thought to himself.

He began wielding his thunderbolt like a sword, practicing the exact moves he had memorised while watching the sword academy located ten blocks away from his house. Suddenly, absolute horror struck. A section of the ground, approximately twenty feet away from Crimson-Shock, began to pulse violently as glowing, greenish veins covered the dirt. Within thirty seconds, the ground erupted, hatching open like a massive egg.

Out of it stepped a bizarre creature. Nathan's brilliant mind instantly began assessing the threat, quickly analyzing his next move. "Five feet tall, so I will be neck-and-neck with him," Nathan calculated. "Humanoid figure, so it will be easy to combat."

The creature began advancing towards Crimson-Shock in a deeply creepy manner, but Nathan stood completely fearless. "If I hold my thunderbolt horizontally at a perfect ninety-degree angle and rush towards him, I will be able to sever his head."

Crimson-Shock locked into his stance, ready to strike while the monster stood twelve feet away. Then, he went for it. In a flash, he executed a flawless horizontal chop. Thick, green blood erupted from the creature like a volcano. Its lifeless body collapsed to the earth and instantly began to decay. Nathan stared at the dissolving pile and thought, "Well, now I know exactly what kind of monsters I am going to be facing." He paused, a small smirk crossing his face. "At least I won't have to clean up after his body, since it is completely biodegradable."

He journeyed back home, trying his absolute best to remain calm and restrain himself from screaming out loud that he had just defeated a literal devil that looked like it wore plants for clothes. He walked through his front door, ate dinner, and finally went to bed, sleeping restlessly as his mind raced toward the future.

Nathan went to school with an aware mood, closely observing everything that was happening around him just in case another monster decided to pop up. He had even done some secret research by taking a small sample of the creature's skin before it fully decayed. Back in his room, he realised that the energy filling the monster was completely opposite to his own; the moment the monster's skin and his Crimson-Shock sparks came near each other, they reacted aggressively. That made perfect scientific sense.

His school day once again went super cheerfully, as he finally had real friends to talk to. Filled with excitement, he went home and eagerly told his father, "Dad, I finally have friends! Aren't you proud of me?"

"Why should I care?" his dad replied coldly.

He went silently to his room, resting up so he could go out and practice. That was when his room suddenly filled with a thick, blue fog, and the exact same eerie music began playing as the last time Aurelius had appeared. But this time, something felt completely different; it wasn't Aurelius, nor was it a common monster. Another deity materialised, but while his body looked like Aurelius's translucent form containing the universe, it was pitch black, as if there was absolutely no light left inside it. Nathan knew it instantly—this was Acheron.

Nathan got his guard up ready and immediately transformed into Crimson-Shock. Acheron stepped forward and let out a deep resonance. "Well, my brother certainly has a very good choice in warriors. You seem to be getting a hold of your power."

Crimson-Shock demanded, "Are you Acheron? If yes, what do you want?"

Acheron flashed a villainous smile. "Oh, nothing much. Just you."

Crimson-Shock asked shakily, "What do you mean?"

Acheron replied smoothly, "I want you to help me devour this planet. No one in this cruel world likes you anyway. My team will give you the true respect you deserve."

Crimson-Shock stood his ground, speaking with absolutely no fear, "No. I am a loyal warrior of Aurelius. I will not join you, ever in eternity!"

Acheron practically shook with raw anger. "I do not take rejections well. Since I cannot harm you directly in this world, I will send something down your path the likes of which you have never seen before. Terror will rise in your area. Beware."

With that final threat, the entity vanished. Since that fateful night, Crimson-Shock vowed to gain complete, flawless control over both of his elemental powers as soon as humanly possible.
