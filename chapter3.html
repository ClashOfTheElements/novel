CHAPTER THREE

The Recruiting

Written by Nizkar Storm

Nathan was doing remarkably well after being discharged from the hospital. Following the battle at the high school, news had spread all over the world that elemental humans could pose a massive global threat. However, the world government publicly disagreed with the panic, choosing to allow them to stay free unless they caused any actual havoc.

"One week left to go, heh," Kage remarked while training alongside Nathan on the open ground. Nathan's fatal physical injuries had recovered well, but the tragic death of his family still deeply traumatised him. Despite the heavy grief, he had managed to return to his normal behaviour, which was all he and Kage truly cared about for now.

Out of sudden curiosity, Nathan asked, "Hey, Moony, you never actually told me how you got your powers." Kage looked over at him and smirked. "Honestly, it's hard to remember. This Aurelius god came to me and said these exact words: 'An internal deity possessing primordial elemental forces, sent to hand them down to a chosen few. I do not have much time, so I will make this brief. My twin brother, Acheron, has surrendered to the dark side. He is actively shattering planets across the cosmos. Because he cannot manifest his cosmic powers directly on your world, he deploys deadly organisms and grants them his fraction of power. You humans are in grave, immediate danger. Therefore, I grant you dominion over a shiny, energetic moon aura.'"

Nathan blinked. "That's almost the exact same thing that happened to me." Kage let out a sudden laugh. "I don't think so, because Acheron actually came to me before Aurelius did. He initially handed me the power of Blood, but then paused, looked at me, and said, 'Sorry, wrong person. I shall devour you soon enough, though.'"

Nathan laughed under his breath. "Wow, he must be really dumb." Kage shook his head, his tone shifting. "You can't imagine how confused and terrified I was in that moment."

Suddenly, a realization struck Nathan. He locked eyes with Kage. "You dummy... think about it! It means that someone else out there actually received those Blood powers, and they are definitely not on our side." Kage stood completely frozen on the training field, utterly astonished by the deduction. Nathan and Kage were natural rivals who competed in absolutely everything, so Nathan grinned widely. "That's 1-0 to me, dude."

Kage and Nathan had a major football tournament incoming, but they felt entirely prepared for it. That was until Nathan suddenly froze and blurped out, "Kage... our first football match is this Sunday!"

Kage gasped, "WHAT?!"

Nathan replied coolly, "Don't sweat it. Just remember absolutely not to use your powers during the game. People are already suspicious, and they will surely try to blame us for cheating if they see anything weird. The thing I'm actually worried about right now is finding our allies."

Kage smirked, trying to lighten the mood. "Now you're the one sweating it. The two of us are more than enough to handle anything." Nathan quietly recalled the god Aurelius strictly instructing him to find the others, but Kage interrupted his thoughts. "Hey, why don't you come over to my place for dinner tonight? My parents are dying to meet you."

Nathan paused, swallowed by a brief wave of loneliness, before offering a small smile. "It wouldn't be bad to have someone make me feel at home again." Kage's face softened with deep sympathy, but he quickly maintained his energetic tone. "Come fast then! I'll have to run ahead and inform them you're coming over, or else we'll definitely have a dinner shortage."

Nathan giggled softly, a rare moment of joy since the tragedy. After stopping by his temporary quarters to freshen up, he headed straight to Kage’s house. He warmly greeted Kage's elders upon arriving and sat down at the dining table. Looking around the brightly lit room, Nathan asked softly, "Wait, I can actually sit at your dining table? Normally, at my house, I just ate on the floor."

Kage felt a pang of sadness for his friend's past, but smiled warmly as he passed a plate. "Hey, I told you. Make yourself completely at home."

Kage's parents warmly offered Nathan a place to stay at their home for the rest of his life, but Nathan gently rejected it, feeling a deep, internal sense of shame. Instead, the rest of the week passed slowly inside his lonely apartment. It wasn't until Saturday night that Kage offered him another home-cooked dinner, arguing that they both desperately needed to fuel up for tomorrow's tournament. He wasn't wrong. Nathan ate the delicious meal and headed back to his room to sleep, mentally preparing for the big day ahead.

The tournament match was scheduled for 7:00 AM sharp, with a mandatory reporting time of 6:30 AM. Nathan woke up at six in the morning, brushed his teeth, and washed his weary soul in the shower. He pulled on his official team kit and hurried over to Kage’s house to catch a ride in their car. By the time they arrived at the tournament grounds, the rest of their team was already waiting anxiously. After a quick warm-up session, the whistle blew, and the match started exactly on time.

Nathan and Kage were playing right at the front as the main strikers. Nathan started at the center circle and immediately passed the ball back to his midfielder. The midfielder dribbled hard until an opposing defender aggressively snatched it away, initiating a rapid succession of passes that caught Nathan's team off-guard and resulted in a quick goal against them.

Determined to bounce back, Kage started the next play by passing clean to Nathan. Nathan activated his exceptional footwork, effortlessly cutting past his opponents. Calculating the physics on the fly, Nathan realized the shooting angle from his current position was too narrow to guarantee a goal, so he executed a perfect cross-pass to Kage instead. Kage timed it beautifully and scored!

"Half-time!" the referee bellowed. Nathan quickly instructed the rest of the squad to stick strictly to their pre-planned defensive strategy before pulling Kage aside for a private huddle. "Don't get distracted during the match, but..." Nathan began, only for both of them to simultaneously whisper in unison, "Taimen is one of the Aurements." They stared at each other with wide, excited eyes before running back onto the field.

They managed to score another goal to secure the win, but neither of them was truly celebrating the victory. Their minds were entirely focused on the fact that a new member was about to join their ranks. The moment the match concluded, they walked directly up to Taimen. "Hey, GG," Kage said. "We wanted to ask you something." Taimen, who clearly disliked socializing, replied coldly, "What do you want? I'm exhausted."

Kage remained perfectly calm. "Do you happen to possess something... mysterious?" Taimen paused, his eyes narrowing. "Yeah. And I know you two do, too." Nathan nodded. "We figured as much. Why don't you come along with us so we can discuss it safely?" Realizing he needed answers just as badly as they did, Taimen muttered, "Fine. I don't really have a choice anyway."

As they began to walk away, Kage suddenly sensed an unfamiliar energy aura nearby. "Hey, Nathan, why is that girl over there staring at us so intently?" Nathan glanced over. "She must be from a different sports section. But now that you mention it, I can feel it too—she is definitely part of us." Taimen gave a slight, affirming nod, raising his hand to signal the mysterious girl to join their group. Kage nudged Nathan's shoulder with a grin. "Looks like the score is 1-1 now, dude!"

Kira followed them without any fuss; she also knew for a fact that they possessed extraordinary powers. All four of them quickly navigated to Nathan's favorite spot, their secret training ground. Right beneath it, they had constructed a hidden underground bunker where absolutely no one else was allowed.

The moment they stepped inside, Kira started, "We need to hurry. Something disastrous is going to happen today. Quick, mention all of your powers." Nathan and Kage briefly exchanged cautious looks. Taimen sighed lazily, "What now?"

"Okay, if something bad is about to happen, Kira and Taimen will be the ones to go for it first," Nathan said strategically. Both Kira and Taimen understood exactly why he made that choice: it was a perfect test to prove whether they were trustworthy, to see if they acknowledged their powers well, and to rapidly strengthen their teamwork. Kage added rushingly, "We will act as your backup if something goes wrong, but we absolutely have to know each other's full abilities first."

Nathan briefly manifested his thunder and fire forms. "Crimson-Shock and Blaze," he declared. Kage went next, stepping forward confidently. "Moon powers." Kira followed up smoothly, "Lumen for my light powers, and Riptide for my water powers." Taimen went last, muttering flatly, "Shadow... with, well, obviously shadow powers."

"Very creative," Blaze remarked with a slight smirk. He continued, his tactical mind analyzing the synergy, "Lumen and Moondust possess completely opposite auras, but Riptide could become incredibly powerful with just Moondust in her presence, because the moon literally controls the ocean tides on Earth." Kira was completely shocked by his high level of scientific intelligence.

Suddenly, all four of them felt a sharp chill as a massive spike of opposing dark energy filled the room. Moving at the speed of light, Lumen vanished, immediately reappearing right behind the source of the incoming energy signature. Moondust and Taimen quickly rushed after her. "We have to race!" Crimson-Shock yelled as they sprinted toward the perimeter. Kage complained loudly as they ran, "How come you get to have two distinct powers anyway?!"

The battle arena was a wide, open ground surrounding an abandoned industrial site. Crimson-Shock and Moondust fearlessly pushed straight inside the main facility, while Lumen and Shadow held their guard up outside, securing the perimeter perfectly.

"Yo, Moony, do you think they are actually powerful?" Crimson-Shock whispered while laying low inside the rusted machinery of the factory.

"They have to be way more powerful than standard Gloom Crawlers if they're daring enough to face us," Moondust replied, pointing through a broken window toward a boy sitting casually on a tree branch outside. The newcomer wore the exact same warrior dress style as the others, but completely in a dark green color palette.

"You don't think he has leaf powers, right?" Lumen asked, hyping herself up for a fight. Standing beside her, Shadow quietly manifested a majestic shadow tiger—its body was black as death with burning, fiery red eyes, completely coiled to strike. On command, the beast sprinted forward and hopped effortlessly up the trunk of the massive tree, which Crimson-Shock calculated to be nearly fifty feet tall!

The leaf-powered boy simply leaned back, falling completely off the branch. For a split second, everyone wondered why he had just dropped to his doom. But mid-fall, he shot a thick, organic vein directly out of his palm, hooking it securely around the branch to swing to safety. "Close call," he smirked, referring to a lethal spike of darkness Shadow had secretly released from below to impale him. Shadow brought his tiger back, absorbing the creature's dark essence back into his open hand.

The opposing warrior landed smoothly on the turf. "The name's Death-Vine. I was just wandering by when I spotted the four of you. I heard that Terra-Voss was recently defeated by some red-shirt rookie."

"Wait, how does he know exactly where we are if we're hidden deep inside the facility and he's outside?" Moondust asked worryingly.

"Because of this thing," Crimson-Shock muttered, stepping out into the light while holding a small, glowing Venus fly-trap in his hands. "This spy plant has been tracking us the entire time."

Lumen bared her teeth. "Enough talking. You're dead!" She charged headfirst out of the building, wielding a highly concentrated, glowing rod of pure light energy. Death-Vine quickly delayed her advance, causing a wall of thick, thorny veins to erupt out of the dirt directly in front of her path.

Death-Vine suddenly noticed that Shadow was completely missing from the ground. He looked up, spotting a massive shadow eagle soaring through the clouds. The bird was one of Shadow's personal 'Defints', as he liked to call his dark summons. Shadow was hanging tightly onto the massive talons of the eagle, dropping down and missing by just an inch as he attempted to drive a heavy kick straight into Death-Vine's face.

Suddenly, a low tremor shook the industrial site. Crimson-Shock's eyes widened as a horde of two thousand Medium-class Gloom Crawlers burst through the perimeter fences, charging directly toward them. Moondust grinned under his breath, turning to his rival. "Hey, Crimson! Let's see who can clear out the most!"

Lumen was still furiously slashing through the endless walls of thorny wood in her way. However, Death-Vine had secretly manipulated his roots beneath the surface; thick vines suddenly erupted and tightly wrapped Shadow against the trunk of the fifty-foot tree. Everyone was far too busy fighting off the horde to notice the trap. Shadow desperately tried to use his hand signs to summon another Defint, but Death-Vine anticipated the move and made sure to bind his arms securely as well. "Weaklings," the villain taunted, looking down upon them.

Noticing her ally in trouble, Lumen double-tapped the side of her cap, causing a pair of cool, orange tactical glasses to instantly snap down in front of her eyes. She squinted through the lenses, rapidly scanning the structural weak points of the plant walls to find the fastest path to the villain. Activating her glowing light palms, she began aggressively burning the organic matter away. She tilted her head back and yelled, "Lens Beam!" shooting two intense rays of laser-like light directly out of her eyes, but the thick, ancient wood of Death-Vine's defenses barely even singed.

Over on the main field, Crimson-Shock vaporized one final, extra Gloom Crawler, officially securing the victory in his kill-count competition against Moondust. But as the smoke cleared, his triumph vanished. He looked over toward the tree and saw just how brutally their brand-new teammates were suffering.

"Don't even think about helping them yet," Moondust said, his voice tightening with sudden, serious confidence as he stood his ground against the closing horde. Crimson-Shock, his heart pounding with fear for his new friends, gripped his thunderbolt tightly. "I know," he replied heavily. Was this truly going to be the tragic end of the allies they had only just found?

"What in the world am I seeing?!" Crimson-Shock yelled, looking up at the sky. A new girl was floating sky-high above the facility, a vibrant pink energy aura radiating intensely from her open hands. She aggressively slammed her palms down against the empty air.

"What the heck did that even do?" Crimson-Shock asked goofily, blinking in confusion. Moondust squinted through the dust and replied, "Look! It's another Aurement... and look at Death-Vine!"

Down on the turf, Death-Vine was suddenly pinned flat against the dirt, the localized gravity crushing down on him so hard he couldn't even lift his head. The mysterious girl raised her hands into the sky, collecting a massive amount of kinetic energy. Death-Vine could only watch in terror, completely paralyzed. Rocketing downward, she charged straight at him and delivered a devastating, gravity-empowered punch directly into his back.

The impact felt as if a massive earthquake had just occurred. Instantly, every single thorny vein holding Shadow and Lumen vanished into thin air, and the dark life completely left Death-Vine's broken stone armor.

Shadow dropped safely to the grass, dusting off his gear. "Hey, my shadow-rabbit was almost about to chew through those vines and release me anyway," he muttered casually.

The floating girl landed, her pink aura fading as she suddenly began to panic. "Oh no! Did I just kill him?! Oh my god, I'm a murderer! I deserve to go straight to hell!" Everyone looked at her, started giggling at the sudden shift in tone, and safely deactivated their warrior forms back into their normal human clothes.

"By the way," she added, trying to calm her breathing, "I'm Nova-Pull. Or just Hazel when I'm normal." Taimen stepped forward, offering a rare, polite nod. "Nice to meet you." Kira immediately leaned over and whispered to Nathan, "He has literally never been that nice to anyone before."

The five teenagers headed straight back to their secret underground bunker, animatedly discussing their superpowers along the way. It turned out Hazel possessed absolute gravity manipulation. But the moment they stepped inside the hideout, they froze in utter surprise. The god Aurelius was standing there, waiting for them in the shadows.

"Very good job on finding all of your allies and successfully defeating two Achelments," Aurelius spoke majestically, his voice echoing through the bunker. Before anyone could ask a single follow-up question, his divine form shattered into particles of light and vanished completely. Kira sighed, crossing her arms. "Of course. Our questions will literally never be answered by that guy."

As the summer break eventually came to a close, a new school year officially began. Nathan and Kage were placed together in the exact same high school section, while their three brand-new allies were enrolled in a different school across town. Walking down the crowded hallway on their very first morning back, Nathan bumped his shoulder against Kage's with a proud, competitive grin. "Hey, it's 2-1 to me now, Kage!"
